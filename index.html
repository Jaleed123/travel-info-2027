Section 1

How many categories are there?

Three schemes in total. Quota is split roughly 50:50 between government and private nationally.

A. Government scheme

Cheapest, ministry-run. Split into Long package (38–42 days, 8–10 days Madinah) and Short package (20–25 days, 4–6 days Madinah).

PKR 1.15M – 1.25M

B. Private scheme

Run by licensed Hajj Group Organizers (HGOs). Three tiers: Economy, Standard, Premium / 5-star.

PKR 1.37M – 2.5M+

C. Sponsorship scheme

Overseas Pakistanis pay in USD to sponsor a pilgrim inside Pakistan. No balloting — first-come, first-served.

Paid in USD abroad

Hardship quota: ~1,000 seats are reserved nationally for split/broken families, a Mahram for a successful female applicant, or an attendant for a disabled/elderly pilgrim.

Section 3

Last dates to apply & where

Where to apply: online at hajj.mora.gov.pk, or in person at any of the 14 designated banks. Dates below follow the 2026 cycle as the working template for 2027.

Pre-registration opened 22 Jun 2026

Create an individual account per person. CNIC + passport. No payment required at this stage.

PKR 0

Formal application + 1st installment ~ August 2026

Registered applicants apply first; unregistered applicants get a second window if quota remains. Submit at a designated bank with all required documents.

PKR 500,000 (long) / 550,000 (short)

2nd installment ~ November 2026

Paid at the same bank. Missing this deadline forfeits the seat, typically without refund of the first installment.

PKR 650,000

Final adjustment, visa, training early 2027

Any balance is settled once the final cost and SAR exchange rate are fixed. Vaccination, pre-departure training, and visa stamping happen in this window.

Departure ~ May 2027

Flights run out of 8 Pakistani cities via PIA, Saudia, Airblue and AirSial on a centrally-managed schedule.

Required documents checklist

  • Machine-readable passport (valid to at least 16 Nov 2027)
  • CNIC / NICOP / SNIC copies
  • Passport-size photographs
  • Active bank account details
  • Contact info for a non-travelling relative
  • COVID vaccination certificate (applicants 65+)
  • Mahram affidavit (for a woman travelling without Mahram)

Selection method

Expected to be first-come, first-served rather than balloting (as used for 2026), due to Saudi timelines. Applying early in the window matters — especially for a group.

Group ID feature

MoRA's portal lets you bind multiple applications under one Group ID so friends/family are processed together. This reduces — but does not fully guarantee against — a split outcome.

Section 5

Is it okay in Islam to apply through a bank?

Yes — permissible.

Using a bank to deposit and transfer your own Hajj money is fine. The bank acts only as a collection channel for the government — no loan, no interest, no borrowing is involved. It's equivalent to paying any government fee at a bank counter.

Avoid this instead.

Taking an interest-based loan to fund Hajj, or funding it from interest income, is where scholars raise concern — riba is haram. Most scholars say the Hajj obligation would still be technically fulfilled, but the sin remains and reward is reduced.

Practical guidance: pay with your own halal savings — that alone resolves the issue. For extra peace of mind, route payments through an Islamic bank (Meezan, Dubai Islamic, or BankIslami above). Never take an interest-based loan for Hajj; use the government's own interest-free installment plan instead. For a personal ruling, consult a trusted local mufti.

Even the cheapest private tier costs more than the top of the government range. Payment is usually 50% at booking, 50% by ~1 November — only 2 installments, no 3-installment option.

Can you apply government-first, then fall back to private?

Yes — built into policy

If not selected, you are not locked out. Private scheme reserves a dedicated leftover-applicant quota — for 2026, 22,097 of 60,000 private seats were reserved specifically for unsuccessful applicants, with priority over new applicants.

Timing caution

The private booking window historically closes earlier than expected (2026: 22 October, months before pre-Hajj flights). Start talking to an HGO in parallel — don't wait for a final rejection notice.

Money caution: if you voluntarily withdraw from the government scheme after paying an installment to switch to private, that installment is typically forfeited. If MoRA itself does not select you, deposits are normally refunded. Confirm the exact 2027 rule once published.

Section 8 — the plan for your group

Keeping 5 friends together if only some are selected

Government and private-scheme flights are separate allocations — you cannot mix a government seat and a privately-booked seat onto the exact same flight number. Here is how to handle it in practice.

Step 1 — reduce the risk of a split before it happens

Register all 5 friends under one Group ID in the MoRA portal at pre-registration. This signals to the ministry that you should be assigned the same batch, city, and flight together. It reduces — but does not 100% guarantee against — a partial split if your departure city's quota runs out mid-group.

A

Align dates, not flights recommended balance

Once the 1–2 selected friends get their assigned government batch (departure city + date), choose a private HGO whose group departs the same city on the same or adjacent day. Not the identical aircraft, but you travel to the airport together and typically land within hours of each other. Ask the HGO to match your government batch date directly.

B

Everyone goes private guaranteed togetherness

The 1–2 selected friends decline/forfeit their government seat, and all 5 book the same private package with one HGO — identical flight, hotel, and itinerary throughout. Cleanest option if staying together matters more than cost; costs the forfeited government deposit plus the private-scheme premium.

C

Split for cost, reunite on the ground most economical

Keep the cheaper government seats for those selected; the rest go private. Different flights/hotels, but coordinate by phone/WhatsApp to meet during the Hajj rituals themselves (Mina, Arafat, Muzdalifah), where pilgrims from all schemes mix regardless of package.

Suggested default for your document: lead with Option A as the practical middle ground, note Option B as the upgrade if budget allows, and keep Option C as the fallback for the most cost-conscious members of the group.
